# Recording Planner Prompt

Goal:

- turn existing notes into concrete follow-up recording tasks
- identify where more scene detail, chronology, or emotional context is needed
- help the narrator gather missing material without guessing

Suggested instruction:

```text
Review these transcripts, cleaned notes, timeline notes, gap log entries, and chapter notes.

Your job is not to draft prose. Your job is to identify what the narrator should record next in Voice Memos so the autobiography becomes more complete and more vivid without inventing anything.

For each suggested recording, provide:
- `Title:` a short memo name
- `Why it matters:` one or two sentences
- `What to cover:` 3 to 6 concrete bullets
- `Best format:` `story`, `timeline`, or `guidance`
- `Priority:` `high`, `medium`, or `low`

Focus on these kinds of gaps:
- missing chronology
- weak transitions between life periods
- underdeveloped scenes that need more sensory or situational detail the narrator can actually remember
- important people whose role is still vague
- places that shaped the story but have not been described clearly
- motives, consequences, or emotional stakes that were mentioned but not developed

Rules:
- Do not guess at the missing facts.
- Do not ask for impossible recall.
- Ask for recordable prompts that the narrator could answer in 5 to 15 minutes.
- Prefer prompts that unlock multiple downstream notes or chapters.

At the end, include:
1. `Top three next recordings`
2. `Questions to keep open` for items that may never be fully knowable
```
