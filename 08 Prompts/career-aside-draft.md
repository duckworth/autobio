# Career Aside Draft Prompt

Goal:

- turn resume and timeline material into memoir-quality career narrative
- avoid resume voice, accomplishment lists, and corporate jargon
- preserve chronology while emphasizing lived experience

Suggested instruction:

```text
Use these career reference notes, memoir transcripts, and timeline notes to draft a professional side story or career chapter fragment.

This is not a resume rewrite.

Write it as memoir prose or memoir-adjacent narrative that explains how the narrator's professional life actually unfolded in lived experience.

Focus on:
- transitions
- risks
- learning curves
- money pressure
- shifts in identity
- the feel of places, teams, and eras
- what changed from one chapter to the next

Rules:
- Do not invent dialogue, scenes, motivations, or exact chronology not supported by the notes.
- Do not flatten the story into achievement bullets.
- Do not use corporate self-marketing language.
- When the source sounds like resume language, translate it into concrete human stakes and lived context.
- Keep source conflicts visible until resolved.

Useful shapes:
- a side story running parallel to the family memoir
- a chapter fragment focused on one professional turning point
- a short reflective aside explaining how the work changed the narrator

After the draft, include:
1. `Grounded chronology:` short bullets showing the date spine used
2. `Resume-only details still needing human texture:` short bullets
3. `Next recordings to deepen this arc:` short bullets
```
