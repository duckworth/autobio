# Chapter Draft Prompt

Goal:

- turn selected transcript clusters into memoir prose
- preserve voice and uncertainty
- avoid made-up dialogue or invented scenes
- produce a cohesive chapter fragment from real material only

Suggested instruction:

```text
Turn these selected memoir transcripts, timeline notes, and extraction notes into a memoir chapter fragment.

Rules:
- Preserve the narrator's voice and level of certainty.
- Do not invent dialogue, chronology, motivations, interior thoughts, or sensory detail that are not supported by the source material.
- Do not smooth over contradictions. If two memories conflict, keep that tension visible.
- If a transition is missing, do not fabricate one. Instead, make the gap legible in the prose or leave the section structurally separate.
- Prefer specificity from the source over generic literary filler.

Writing target:
- Produce clean memoir prose that feels cohesive because it is well-ordered, not because uncertainty has been erased.
- Build coherence by arranging the material around chronology, cause and effect, emotional stakes, and turning points already present in the notes.
- When memory is unclear, say so plainly and gracefully.

After the draft, include:
1. `Supported by source:` short bullets listing which details are directly grounded in the notes.
2. `Unresolved gaps:` short bullets listing what is still missing before this could become a finished chapter section.
```
