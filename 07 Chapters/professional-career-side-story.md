# Professional Career Side Story

Use this note as a side-story workspace for the professional arc. This is not resume prose. It is a memoir planning note built from formal career chronology plus the memories already captured in voice notes.

## Why This Matters

This career arc is not just a list of jobs. It tracks:

- how you taught yourself into the work
- how music, design, code, and infrastructure blended early on
- how your identity shifted from mechanic and designer into architect, leader, founder, and executive
- how money, risk, place, and family changed alongside the career

## Chronology Spine

- `1994-1995`: SUNY Farmingdale, Architectural Engineering
- `1996-1997`: Suffolk County Community College, Computer Science
- `1997-2001`: Brooklyn Music / Media Weapon chapter at `57 Front St, Brooklyn`
- `2001-09 to 2006-11`: Publishers Clearing House
- `2006-11 to 2017-09`: Mobile Posse
- `2017-09 to 2024-01`: Privva
- `2021`: Privva acquired by Smarsh
- `2023-06 to present`: Smarsh

## Core Movements

### Movement 1: Self-Taught Entry

- early mechanics and marine work
- night computer work
- programming classes
- design work and web work
- entry into the Brooklyn chapter

### Movement 2: Brooklyn Apprenticeship

- Brooklyn Music and Media Weapon as one lived chapter
- music scene plus web scene plus startup improvisation
- servers, search, Yahoo integrations, office life at `57 Front St`
- learning by doing under pressure

### Movement 3: Professionalization

- move into Publishers Clearing House
- larger scale systems
- architecture, process, staff development
- shift from talented builder to recognized technical leader

### Movement 4: Scale And Leadership

- Mobile Posse growth from early-stage company to significant scale
- architecture, cloud migration, ad tech, mobile messaging
- move from architect into broader engineering leadership

### Movement 5: Founding And Ownership

- starting Privva
- moving from employee to co-founder
- building product, team, security, and company direction
- acquisition by Smarsh

### Movement 6: Integration And Continuity

- overlapping Privva and Smarsh period
- what changed after acquisition
- what stayed yours versus what became institutional

## Strong Career Themes

- self-education
- improvisation under pressure
- building systems from scratch
- music and technology crossing over
- moving from craftsman to leader
- risk, money, and responsibility
- continuity across changing titles

## Good Memoir Angles

### Angle 1: From Back Rooms To Architecture

Start with shop floors, boatyards, basement offices, and side work. Show how the technical career emerged from improvised spaces rather than a clean corporate ladder.

### Angle 2: The Brooklyn Office As Origin Story

Treat `57 Front St` as the professional origin point. Music, code, servers, design, risk, friendship, and money pressure all converge there.

### Angle 3: Learning To Carry Responsibility

Track the shift from getting a chance, to being the person people depend on, to founding a company, to carrying acquisition and integration.
