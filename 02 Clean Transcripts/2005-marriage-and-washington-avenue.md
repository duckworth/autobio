I was living in an apartment with my dad on Bristol Street in Islip Terrace when I met Samantha. We met through Match.com after I found her message in my spam folder while I was in North Carolina visiting my mom. We hit it off by email and set up a date when I got back to New York.

Our first date was September 20, 2003 at Milk and Sugar in Bay Shore. After dinner we went mini-golfing in Oakdale. I remember leaving that date in a daze because it felt immediately different. After that we talked constantly, spent time together, and built the relationship over about 18 months.

During that period we went away on small trips, including upstate New York and Vermont. I showed her my uncle's old Vermont cabin in the woods, and later I proposed during a Vermont bed-and-breakfast weekend.

We got married in Turks and Caicos on October 17, 2005. We stayed at Royal West Indies and had to be on the island long enough beforehand to complete the paperwork. Samantha's parents were there. After the wedding a tropical storm hit and knocked out power across much of the island, so the next few days were rougher than planned, with rolling power and improvised meals, but the trip was still beautiful.

When we got back, Samantha tried to update her name at the DMV, but they would not accept the Turks and Caicos marriage paperwork. We then had to go to Islip Town Hall to have the marriage recognized formally in the United States, which is why the official certificate date does not match the actual wedding date.

Around that same period we moved into my grandmother's house on Washington Avenue in Islip Terrace before formally buying it from my Uncle Bob, Robert Lemp, my father's brother. We completed the purchase in 2005 and moved into the main part of the house. We fixed it up as best we could, did landscaping and gardening, put up an above-ground pool, and began building a life there together.
